1  SNES Controller Communication Protocol {#protocol}
2  ======================================
3 
4  This section describes the SNES controller protocol and was copied from
5  the Gamefaqs website.
6 
7  > Every 16.67ms (or about 60Hz), the SNES CPU sends out a 12us wide, positive
8  > going data latch pulse on pin 3. This instructs the ICs in the controller
9  > to latch the state of all buttons internally. Six microsenconds after the
10  > fall of the data latch pulse, the CPU sends out 16 data clock pulses on
11  > pin 2. These are 50% duty cycle with 12us per full cycle. The controllers
12  > serially shift the latched button states out pin 4 on every rising edge
13  > of the clock, and the CPU samples the data on every falling edge.
14  >
15  > Each button on the controller is assigned a specific id which corresponds
16  > to the clock cycle during which that button's state will be reported.
17  > The table in section 4.0 lists the ids for all buttons. Note that
18  > multiple buttons may be depressed at any given moment. Also note
19  > that a logic "high" on the serial data line means the button is NOT
20  > depressed.
21  >
22  > At the end of the 16 cycle sequence, the serial data line is driven low
23  > until the next data latch pulse. The only slight deviation from this
24  > protocol is apparent in the first clock cycle. Because the clock is
25  > normally high, the first transition it makes after latch signal is
26  > a high-to-low transition. Since data for the first button (B in this
27  > case) will be latched on this transition, it's data must actually be
28  > driven earlier. The SNES controllers drive data for the first button
29  > at the falling edge of latch. Data for all other buttons is driven at
30  > the rising edge of clock. Hopefully the following timing diagram will
31  > serve to illustrate this. Only 4 of the 16 clock cycles are shown for
32  > brevity.
